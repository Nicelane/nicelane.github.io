Identify Values

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I'm aroreretini (but have yet to lean into the medical system to gain an official diagnoses) with it comes a strong moral compass, and insane level of rightenous.

Having worked in the arts often artists are just happy doing what they love without monetary value. Sometimes this comes with certain conditioning that they should just be happy getting what they're given. Like many people within society often they can't be bothered by the small print of contracts and agreements, and just want to get on with things. This can especially be the case when these documents are written in legalese.

When when of the artists I work with received funding from the NZ Arts Council, they put additional conditions on it. We responding with our own conditions, including having the contract in an official format known as Easy Read. After two years of Human Rights Proceedings we ending up paving the way for plain language contracts and new policy for disabled artists to gain sovereighnty within arts funding in Aotearoa.

Reflecting on the decision, many of our peers at the time suggested taking the money and just getting on with it. I believe our self sacrifice helped shift the funding culture for those behind us, but it came at a financial and emotional toll that shouldn't have been there in the first place if international law was followed.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

I've been priviledged to grow up in Aotearoa, and benefit from living on the shores of Te Moana Nui a Kiwa.

Raised by white Canadian immigrants they gave me a foundation of holding to and advocating for principles that I believe in. Both my parents have a strong sense of hustle, and while my mum doesn't appreciate me saying it, I was raised in the strong north american tradiional of capitalism.

Feminism has always been present in my life, but my parents shied away from outwardly identifying themselves as such, in my earlier life my mum did a lot more of the parenting, but as I turned into a teenager she shifted into more of a breadwinner role and my father got to take up house husband status.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

When focused I can be a non-stop workhorse. I love problem solving and coming up with different, unique and innovative solutions. Working in group is super enjoyable as well as attempting to enable everybody to feel like they can share their point of view.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

Having worked in a self driven space, often on projects that I'm super passionate about, one of my fears is when work isn't that exciting questioning how much I want to lean into it. As a producer I've often been in the 'nobody else wants to do it' gap-filler type roles, this has pushed me into a more generalised skill set. At the moment I'm keen to lean into more hard and specialised skills, which would help to round out everything else I bring to the table.

Balancing time is probably my biggest limitation, as a people pleaser and person who often leans into community, I can often sacrifice myself by saying yes to too many things, ensuring I'm prioritising myself will probably be the hardest thing.

As I've gotten older I'm more into leaning into the auntie approach to dealing with struggles, talking through things to get the the heart of the issue, and attmepting to adjust, and remove pain points.
